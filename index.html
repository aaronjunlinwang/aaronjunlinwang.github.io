Chinese Anti-Trust Investigation Gives Us a Good Opportunity to Invest (More) Into Tencent

Posted on 2020-12-28 Edited on 2020-12-29 In Investment Analysis

Tl;dr: Anti-trust investigation should not hinder long term growth of Tencent.

Since November 2020, there were rumours about Chinese regulators investigating anti-trust issues within the tech industry. On December 23, Chinese regulators put Alibaba under investigation by regulators, which caused its stock price to plunge and affected other Chinese tech firms such as Tencent and Meituan. The market has somewhat a mutual consensus that the Chinese regulators will give them the same treatment, in one way or another. Yet, it is pointless to guess what the regulators will do. Instead, we should focus on does the anti-trust investigation and potential punishments hurt Tencent?

To answer this question, we first should understand the fundamentals of Tencent.
In short, fundamentally, Tencent is a social media/communication company focused on connecting family, friends, and acquaintances. By connecting them on Wechat/QQ, Tencent may generate business revenue through various means such as games, mobile payment, investment, etc. As described by the image above, Communication & Social is Tencent’s driving force for all other revenue-generating services.

Moat

At its core, Weixin, WeChat, and QQ have a collective MAU of 1.83 Billion (with overlapping users). This possibly makes one of the widest moats in the business world. Thus, unless there is another next-level communication product that will replace Wechat (which I believe won’t be another instant messenger) or the regulators decided to split up Tencent by taking Wechat out of the company, Tencent investors shouldn’t be worried.

FinTech Worries

There are particular worries about the FinTech sector of Tencent. On Dec 18th, in the Chinese Central Economic Work Conference, one of the key missions of 2021 is “Strengthen anti-trust measures and prevent disorderly expansion of capital.” From what we have already seen in Ant Group’s suspended IPO, the Chinese government is cautious and conservative regarding the financial industry. As we say, the nail that sticks out gets hammered down. Ant Group’s market-leading position and bold steps in the financial sector are some of the factors that caused this incident. Besides, Ant Group’s nature of acting as a financial intermediary to provide loans, credit payments, and manage savings together with 100+ banks and thousands of financial institutions that it is partnered with within China. The Central bank targeted this attempt to change the financial industry in the Commercial Banking Law proposal published on Oct 16th, 2020, which was two months after Ant Group’s IPO announcement and weeks before its IPO suspension. Article 52 mentioned specialised development of banks:

Commercial banks should establish specialised development strategies based on their types, scales, and practices. Regional commercial banks such as City commercial banks, Rural commercial banks should only operate legally in their domiciles; they may not operate in other areas without permission.

This is highly related as the head of Central Bank’s Financial Stability Board has mentioned in November and December that

Regional Banks are attracting nationwide deposits through platforms of internet goliaths and becoming defacto National Banks.”

Thus, to preventing systematic risks, FinTech platforms are hammered down, and amongst them, Ant Group is the tallest nail.

Tencent would still take a hit on its wealth management and micro-lending business (LiCaiTong); it is a lucrative business because it is a direct income from financial services, unlike indirect income such as advertisement on contents.

Therefore, from the perspective of financial regulations, Tencent may experience low growth in its wealth management and micro-lending business. However, it doesn’t stop it from introducing new products and earn money from other more popular products and services.

Management

It comes down to Tencent’s management at the end of the day. One of the key reasons why we invested in Tencent was its excellent and trustworthy management. I will explain more about their management in future posts; it is essential to know that they are product-oriented people with reliable values.

Conclusion

Tencent is a solid business, and the stock price is being driven down by fear while its fundamentals are staying constant.
